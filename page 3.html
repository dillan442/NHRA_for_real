Who is Wally Parks?

Wally Parks, born on January 23, 1913 and died on September 28, 2007, is the founder of The National Hot Rod Association. He started out as a writer for automobile magazines, and was the co-founder of as Hot Rod magazine and also played a huge part in the founding of the motor trend magazine. In these magazines, his top priority was safety and he backed up his claim by creating safety safaris, or safety crews that would go around the United States and teach people about organized and safe racing. This was how he got people off the streets and onto the track.

In the 1950s, he officially founded the NHRA which is the largest motorsport bodies in the history of all racing whether its Drag Racing, Nascar, or Formula racing. Wally Parks even went to Australia and England to promote safety in Drag Racing.
